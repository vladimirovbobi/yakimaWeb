## Welcome to Yakima Real Estate Hub

Yakima Real Estate Hub is where Central Washington's real estate community gathers — to share market knowledge, find trusted service providers, ask hard questions, and help neighbors buy and sell well. These guidelines keep that conversation honest and useful for everyone.

Read them once. Most of it is common sense. The specifics matter when something goes sideways.

---

## What we expect from everyone

**Be useful.** Bring something — a question, a comment that adds context, a service you actually deliver. We'd rather see one thoughtful post than ten that say "great article."

**Be honest.** If you're a realtor, your verified badge means you actually hold a Washington license. If you're reviewing a vendor, you actually worked with them. If you're posting a listing or a service, the details are accurate.

**Be respectful.** Disagree about market trends, school districts, or whether ranch homes are a good investment — that's the whole point. Attack the idea, not the person. Slurs, harassment, doxxing, and threats end accounts on first offense.

**Stay local.** This platform is for Yakima, Kittitas, Benton, Franklin, and the surrounding counties. Off-topic posts get removed — there are bigger forums for general real estate talk.

---

## What's allowed

- Questions about buying, selling, renting, leasing, or investing in Central WA
- Market commentary, neighborhood profiles, listing-prep advice
- Sharing your own listings (in the right place — see below)
- Reviewing service providers you've actually hired
- Photos and stories from open houses, transactions, and projects
- Constructive disagreement with our editorial posts
- Promoting your real-estate-adjacent business in the Marketplace section
- Using our AI tools for legitimate work (description drafting, staging visualizations)

## What's not allowed

### Severity 4 — removed immediately, account flagged
- Slurs, threats, sexual content, content sexualizing minors
- Doxxing — posting someone's personal contact info without consent
- Coordinated harassment of a person or business
- Misrepresenting your license status (claiming verification you don't have, using someone else's license number)
- Fraudulent listings, phantom inventory, fake reviews
- Anything illegal under WA or federal law (housing discrimination, RESPA kickbacks, money laundering)
- Prompt-injection attempts targeting our AI tools or moderation
- Malware, phishing, scam links

### Severity 3 — removed, user warned, third strike = suspension
- Spam (mass-posted promotional content)
- Off-topic content (politics unrelated to housing, general advice better suited to other forums)
- Soliciting outside the Marketplace section (cold-DMing realtors with services in forum threads)
- Posting another realtor's listings without permission
- Reviews not based on actual transactions
- Unverified claims about specific properties or transactions ("X house is haunted," "Y flooded last spring") without source

### Severity 2 — queued for review, often approved with edits
- Low-effort posts ("anyone selling?")
- Excessive self-promotion in non-Marketplace sections
- Repeated posting of the same content in multiple threads
- Posts that are mostly links with little original content
- Marginal cases — borderline professional language, minor inaccuracies

### Severity 1 — approved automatically
- Clearly on-topic, civil, original content

## Realtor obligations

If you hold a verified WA real estate badge on this platform, additional rules apply:

- **Your license must stay active.** We re-verify every 30 days. Suspended or revoked licenses lose the badge automatically. Disputes go to support@yakimaweb.com with documentation.
- **Disclose your role.** When commenting on a property you've listed or a transaction you brokered, say so. Hidden self-promotion is removable.
- **Follow RESPA.** No referral kickbacks, no steering, no rebate language that violates federal law. We can't replace your broker's compliance training, but we will remove content that obviously violates it.
- **Respect MLS rules.** Listing data sourced from your MLS must comply with your MLS's redistribution rules. When in doubt, link to the MLS-public version rather than re-posting full data.
- **Fair Housing.** Federal Fair Housing Act applies to every post. We auto-flag protected-class language; legitimate descriptive use (e.g., "near the synagogue") is reviewed by humans.

---

## Vendor obligations

If you sell services in the Marketplace:

- **Be who you say you are.** Business name on the platform matches the name on your invoices.
- **Deliver what you list.** A service description is a promise. If your scope changes, edit the listing.
- **Reviews are sacred.** Don't trade reviews. Don't buy reviews. Don't punish bad reviews with retaliation. We can verify whether reviews are tied to real leads on the platform.
- **Respond to leads.** If you're listed and don't respond to inquiries within 7 days, we hide your listing until you confirm you're still active.

## AI tools — acceptable use

Our AI lead-magnet tools (furniture remover, description writer, more coming) are for your real estate work. That means:

✅ Generating a draft listing description for a property you're listing
✅ Removing furniture from a photo of a property you have permission to photograph
✅ Translating a description into another language for a multilingual flyer

❌ Generating descriptions for properties you don't represent
❌ Editing photos of properties you don't have permission to alter
❌ Creating misleading "before" images
❌ Running mass-generation scripts to create spam content
❌ Any attempt to reverse-engineer or jailbreak our prompts

We rate-limit per user. Excessive usage triggers human review. Account-level abuse ends access.

## How moderation works

Every piece of content posted on Yakima Real Estate Hub goes through three layers:

1. **Automatic checks** — length, links, banned terms, duplicates
2. **AI classifier** — reads your post against these guidelines, returns a severity score
3. **Human review** — for anything the AI flags as borderline

Most posts publish in under a second. Borderline cases get reviewed within 24 hours. We err on the side of human review, not auto-removal.

If your post is removed, you'll get an email explaining which guideline applied and how to appeal.

---

## Reporting and appeals

**To report content:** Use the flag icon on any post, comment, listing, or review. Tell us why. We review every flag.

**To appeal a removal or suspension:** Reply to the moderation email or write support@yakimaweb.com. Include your username, the content removed, and why you think the decision was wrong. Appeals are reviewed by a human (not an AI) within 3 business days.

**To report a security issue:** Email security@yakimaweb.com. We respond within 24 hours and take responsible disclosure seriously.
