Community Guidelines · v1

The rules that keep this useful.

Most of it is common sense. The specifics matter when something goes sideways. Read once. Refer back when you have to.

What we expect from everyone

Be useful. Bring something — a question, a comment that adds context, a service you actually deliver. We'd rather see one thoughtful post than ten that say "great article."

Be honest. If you're a realtor, your verified badge means you actually hold a Washington license. If you're reviewing a vendor, you actually worked with them. If you're posting a listing or a service, the details are accurate.

Be respectful. Disagree about market trends, school districts, or whether ranch homes are a good investment — that's the whole point. Attack the idea, not the person. Slurs, harassment, doxxing, and threats end accounts on first offense.

Stay local. This platform is for Yakima, Kittitas, Benton, Franklin, and surrounding counties. Off-topic posts get removed — there are bigger forums for general real estate talk.

What's allowed

  • Questions about buying, selling, renting, leasing, or investing in Central WA
  • Market commentary, neighborhood profiles, listing-prep advice
  • Sharing your own listings (in the right place — see below)
  • Reviewing service providers you've actually hired
  • Photos and stories from open houses, transactions, and projects
  • Constructive disagreement with our editorial posts
  • Promoting your real-estate-adjacent business in the Marketplace section
  • Using our AI tools for legitimate work (description drafting, staging visualizations)

What's not allowed

Severity 4 — removed immediately, account flagged

  • Slurs, threats, sexual content, content sexualizing minors
  • Doxxing — posting someone's personal contact info without consent
  • Coordinated harassment of a person or business
  • Misrepresenting license status (claiming verification you don't have, using someone else's license number)
  • Fraudulent listings, phantom inventory, fake reviews
  • Anything illegal under WA or federal law (housing discrimination, RESPA kickbacks, money laundering)
  • Prompt-injection attempts targeting our AI tools or moderation
  • Malware, phishing, scam links

Severity 3 — removed, user warned, third strike = suspension

  • Spam (mass-posted promotional content)
  • Off-topic content (politics unrelated to housing, general advice better suited elsewhere)
  • Soliciting outside the Marketplace section (cold-DMing realtors with services in forum threads)
  • Posting another realtor's listings without permission
  • Reviews not based on actual transactions
  • Unverified claims about specific properties or transactions without source

Severity 2 — queued for review, often approved with edits

  • Low-effort posts ("anyone selling?")
  • Excessive self-promotion in non-Marketplace sections
  • Repeated posting of the same content in multiple threads
  • Posts that are mostly links with little original content
  • Marginal cases — borderline professional language, minor inaccuracies

Severity 1 — approved automatically

  • Clearly on-topic, civil, original content

Realtor obligations

If you hold a verified WA real estate badge:

  • Your license must stay active. We re-verify every 30 days. Suspended or revoked licenses lose the badge automatically.
  • Disclose your role. When commenting on a property you've listed or a transaction you brokered, say so.
  • Follow RESPA. No referral kickbacks, no steering, no rebate language that violates federal law.
  • Respect MLS rules. Listing data sourced from your MLS must comply with your MLS's redistribution rules.
  • Fair Housing. Federal Fair Housing Act applies to every post. We auto-flag protected-class language; legitimate descriptive use is reviewed by humans.

Vendor obligations

  • Be who you say you are. Business name on the platform matches the name on your invoices.
  • Deliver what you list. A service description is a promise.
  • Reviews are sacred. No trading, no buying, no retaliation.
  • Respond to leads within 7 days or your listing hides until you confirm you're still active.

AI tools — acceptable use

OK: Generating a draft description for a property you're listing. Removing furniture from a photo of a property you have permission to photograph. Translating a description for a multilingual flyer.

Not OK: Generating descriptions for properties you don't represent. Editing photos of properties you don't have permission to alter. Creating misleading "before" images. Mass-generation scripts. Any attempt to reverse-engineer or jailbreak our prompts.

How moderation works

Every piece of content runs through three layers:

  1. Automatic checks — length, links, banned terms, duplicates.
  2. AI classifier — reads against these guidelines, returns a severity score.
  3. Human review — for anything the AI flags as borderline.

Most posts publish in under a second. Borderline cases get reviewed within 24 hours. We err on the side of human review, not auto-removal.

Reporting + appeals

To report: hit the flag icon on any post, comment, listing, or review. To appeal a removal: reply to the moderation email or write support@yakimaweb.com. Appeals reviewed by a human within 3 business days.

Versioning

These guidelines are versioned. Current version v1, published 2026-05-03. When we change them substantively, we publish the diff, give 14 days notice for tightening, and apply changes only to content posted after the new version takes effect.

We built this so Central Washington has a place that respects your time and your work. If you spot something we missed, tell us — the guidelines improve when the community pushes back.
